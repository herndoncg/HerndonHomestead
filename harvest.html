Herndon Harvest – Garden & Pantry

Herndon Harvest is about planting, growing, and preserving. From canned peaches to seasonal produce, we believe in making the most of every harvest.

Harvest Heart

Plant • Grow • Preserve

We plan with the seasons, build soil with compost, and sow with intention. We tend the garden daily—watering, pruning, and stewarding what the Lord brings to life. Then we preserve: canning, fermenting, freezing, and dehydrating so nothing good is wasted. We aim to use every peach, pepper, and potato well.

Heavenly Harvest

Creation is a gift, and the harvest reminds us of God’s steady provision. We work the soil, but the increase belongs to Him. In all we do we commit our work to the Lord (Proverbs 16:3), trusting Him with the outcome. We grow and gather with gratitude, preserve with wisdom, and share at the table so the fruit of the land becomes a testimony of faith, hospitality, and simple living.

Recipes & How‑Tos

Refrigerator Dill Pickles

Overview

Yield: 2 quartsActive: 20 minReady: 24–48 hrs (refrigerator)

Ingredients

  • 2 lb small cucumbers (Kirby/pickling), scrubbed
  • 2 cups water & 2 cups distilled white vinegar
  • 2 Tbsp kosher or pickling salt
  • 8 sprigs fresh dill
  • 4 cloves garlic, halved
  • 2 tsp mustard seeds, 2 tsp black peppercorns
  • Optional: 1 jalapeño, sliced; pinch red pepper flakes

Tools

  • 2 clean quart jars with lids
  • Small saucepan & ladle, funnel (optional)

  1. 1) Make the brine

    Bring water, vinegar, and salt to a simmer, stirring to dissolve. Turn off heat. (Spices go in the jars, not the pot.)

  2. 2) Pack the jars

    Trim blossom ends; slice spears or coins. Divide dill, garlic, mustard seeds, and peppercorns between jars. Tightly pack cucumbers.

  3. 3) Add brine

    Ladle hot brine over cucumbers to cover. Tap to release bubbles. Add jalapeño or red pepper flakes if using. Cap and cool to room temp.

  4. 4) Chill & enjoy

    Refrigerate 24–48 hours before eating. Keeps 1–2 months refrigerated. Great on sandwiches, burgers, or chopped into relish.

Canning Basics: Peaches (Water‑Bath)

Tools

  • Large water‑bath canner (or deep stockpot) with rack
  • Canning jars (pints or quarts), new lids & bands
  • Jar lifter, canning funnel, bubble remover (or chopstick)
  • Large bowl with ice water, slotted spoon, clean towels

Ingredients

  • Fresh ripe peaches (about 2–2.5 lbs per quart)
  • Light syrup (optional): 1 cup sugar + 4 cups water (approx.)
  • Lemon juice for anti‑browning (optional)

For food safety, always follow tested guidance and adjust processing time for altitude per USDA recommendations.

  1. Prep Jars & Syrup

    Wash jars, lids, and bands. Keep jars hot in simmering water. Simmer syrup in a separate pot.

  2. Peel Peaches

    Score an “X,” blanch 30–60 seconds, then ice‑bath. Slip off skins, pit, and slice. Toss with a little lemon juice if desired.

  3. Hot Pack

    Pre‑heat peach slices in hot syrup a few minutes. Pack hot peaches into hot jars, ladle syrup to 1/2" headspace. Remove bubbles and wipe rims.

  4. Process

    Apply lids/bands fingertip‑tight. Process at a full rolling boil: pints ~20 minutes, quarts ~25 minutes (adjust for altitude).

  5. Cool & Store

    Cool 12–24 hours. Check seals, label, and store in a cool, dark place. Enjoy within a year for best quality.

Fermentation 101: Simple Sauerkraut

Tools

  • 1 wide‑mouth quart jar
  • Fermentation weight (or small jar) & lid/airlock
  • Cutting board & knife

Ingredients

  • 1 medium cabbage (~2 lb)
  • ~1.5 Tbsp non‑iodized salt (≈2% by weight)
  • Optional: caraway seeds

Cleanliness matters. Keep everything rinsed and free of soap residue.

  1. Shred & Salt

    Thinly slice cabbage; sprinkle salt and massage until it releases brine (5–10 minutes).

  2. Pack & Submerge

    Pack tightly into the jar so brine rises to cover. Add weight to keep cabbage submerged.

  3. Ferment

    Cover loosely or use an airlock. Ferment at cool room temperature 1–3 weeks until tangy. Refrigerate to slow fermentation.

Freezing Greens: Spinach, Collards, Kale

Tools

  • Large pot, ice bath, tongs, baking sheet
  • Freezer bags or containers

Prep

  • Wash thoroughly and destem tough ribs.
  • Bring a large pot of water to a boil; prepare an ice bath.

Blanching locks in color and nutrition and prevents bitterness.

  1. Blanch

    Boil 1–2 minutes (collards 3). Transfer immediately to the ice bath.

  2. Cool & Dry

    Drain well; squeeze out excess water. Chop if desired.

  3. Portion & Freeze

    Pre‑freeze portions on a sheet pan, then store in freezer bags. Label and date.

Roasted Garden Salsa

Yield: ~4 cupsActive: 25 minReady: 40 min

Ingredients

  • 2 lb ripe tomatoes, halved
  • 1 jalapeño (2 for hot), whole
  • 1/2 onion, quartered
  • 3 cloves garlic, unpeeled
  • 1/2 cup cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime, salt to taste

  1. Roast

    Broil tomatoes, jalapeño, onion, and garlic on a sheet pan until blistered (8–12 min), turning once.

  2. Blend

    Peel garlic. Pulse everything with cilantro and lime. Season with salt; blend smooth or leave chunky.

  3. Chill

    Chill 30 minutes to let flavors meld. Serve with chips, tacos, or eggs.

Summer Succotash with Okra & Corn

Yield: 4 servingsActive: 20 minReady: 25 min

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh corn kernels
  • 2 cups sliced okra
  • 1 cup halved cherry tomatoes
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 2 Tbsp butter or olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced; 1 tsp fresh thyme
  • Salt & black pepper; splash of apple cider vinegar

  1. Prep

    Cut corn off cobs; slice okra; halve tomatoes; dice onion.

  2. Sauté

    Heat fat; cook onion 2–3 min. Add okra and corn; cook 5–7 min until tender‑crisp. Stir in garlic and thyme 1 min.

  3. Finish

    Fold in tomatoes; season with salt, pepper, and a splash of vinegar. Serve warm.

Seasonal Produce

Summer
  • Tomatoes
    Plant: after last frost (Mar–Apr) • Replant: late Jul for fall
    Spices: basil, oregano, garlic
  • Peppers (bell, jalapeño)
    Transplant into warm soil (May)
    Spices: cumin, paprika, coriander
  • Cucumbers
    Direct sow after frost (Apr–May) • Succession every 2–3 wks
    Spices: dill, mustard seed, garlic
  • Zucchini/Summer squash
    Direct sow after frost • Succession every 3–4 wks
    Spices: thyme, chili flake, lemon zest
  • Okra
    Sow when soil > 70°F (May–Jun)
    Spices: cayenne, garlic, smoked paprika
  • Eggplant
    Transplant when nights > 60°F (May)
    Spices: oregano, sumac, pepper
  • Basil
    Sow/transplant after frost; pinch to bush
    Pairs with tomatoes, garlic, lemon
  • Dill
    Succession sow spring to midsummer
    For pickles; pairs with cucumber, fish
